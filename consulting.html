Consulting

MetamodelR staff have decades of rich expertise in data analytics, ranging from data engineering all the way through to data science. We have worked predominantly in Australian corporations and Government agencies, but also internationally.


Our expertise and core competencies allow us to focus on delivering real value to you without trying to be experts at every aspect of information technology. Since data is the life blood of any organisation, we are embedded right in the core of your operations, delivering ROI to finance, marketing, risk management, research and development divisions, just to name a few. For example, we know that full stack developers, and even data engineers, don't generally have the business and scientific background to provide anything other than business intelligence dashboard type of analytics, when an organisation may actually be after deeper insights from the data through artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, predictive analytics and advanced mathematical and computational modelling. Likewise, we know that various business resources and analysts don't generally have the background to find, access and integrate data if it exists in mainframes, linux servers, parquet files, etc. requiring specialist knowledge of data warehousing and data engineering.


Let MetamodelR connect the data dots for you and provide solutions to your analytics needs.

Technologies Used

Algorithmic trading, also called automated trading, black-box trading, or algo trading, is the use of electronic platforms for entering trading orders with an algorithm which executes pre-programmed trading instructions whose variables may include timing, price, or quantity of the order, or in many cases initiating the order by a system, without human intervention. Algorithmic trading is widely used by investment banks, pension funds, mutual funds, and other buy-side (investor-driven) institutional traders, to divide large trades into several smaller trades to manage market impact and risk. Sell side traders, such as market makers and some hedge funds, provide liquidity to the market, generating and executing orders automatically.
